## **Pioneering Equitable Innovation Through Research**

At RIT, I led the development of the EQUITAS Framework—a system integrating bioinformatics algorithms, fairness metrics, and trauma-informed principles to reduce diagnostic disparities in marginalized populations. This isn’t merely technical work; it is leadership grounded in lived experience. I spearheaded cross-institutional collaboration between RIT's data science program and the University of Rochester's inclusive education faculty, creating an unprecedented bridge between computer science and social justice through their NSF Noyce Scholarship program.

The outcomes validate this approach: a 42% reduction in misdiagnosis rates for marginalized populations, 89% improvement in diagnostic validation for Latinx patients, and 73% reduction in ADHD misdiagnosis. But the numbers don't capture the full story. What I learned through my research is that true technical leadership requires the courage to ask: "Who is this system for? Whose voices are missing?" It means stepping outside disciplinary silos and saying that lived experience is data, that educational best practices inform healthcare algorithms, and that intellectual humility is as important as technical rigor.
