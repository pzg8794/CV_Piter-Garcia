Recommended.

Core idea:
- use RNA-seq or a downstream RNA-based diagnostic workflow as a biologically grounded example of limited context, uneven measurement quality, and fairness risk
- study how sample quality, read depth, coverage, normalization, alignment/reference bias, or biomarker transfer affect downstream diagnostic performance across population groups

Why it is stronger:
- more defensible than "RNA vaccines for different populations"
- closer to diagnostic pipelines and measurement fairness
- aligns better with BIO630, equitable bioinformatics, and the DSCI601 diagnostic-like testbed

### Option 2: Fair RNA biomarker discovery pipeline
Also strong.

Core idea:

Why it works:
- directly aligns with prior equitable bioinformatics work
- easier to connect to diagnostic decision support

### Option 3: RNA vaccines by population
Not recommended as the main framing.

Why not:

## Recommended Scientific Framing
The strongest question to ask Dr. Rodriguez is not:
- "Can RNA vaccines be improved for different races?"

The stronger question is:
- whether RNA-seq or a downstream RNA-based diagnostic pipeline can be used as a realistic fairness testbed
- and whether uneven context, reference bias, sample quality, or biomarker transfer across population groups could lead to different diagnostic performance
