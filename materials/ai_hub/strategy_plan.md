## Objective
This is not a cold application. The goal is to make hiring feel obvious, low-risk, and strategically useful.

The case is:
- You are already a trusted internal contributor in the Travis Desell / Software Engineering / Data Science research orbit.
- You already work in Python-heavy quantum and AI research infrastructure with validated logs, reproducible experiments, documentation, and research tooling.
- The AI Hub role is a natural continuation of that operating mode: real data, careful analysis, reproducibility, and applied AI.
- If secured, the role becomes a bridge to deeper faculty trust, stronger funded research continuity, and a more credible PhD path at RIT.

## Strategic Thesis
The strongest positioning is not "I want this job because I need funding."

The strongest positioning is:
- "I am already producing in RIT research environments that require technical discipline, reproducibility, documentation, and collaboration."
- "I can start quickly with very low supervision cost because I already know how to work inside faculty-led research workflows."
- "My current GA work in quantum and AI research proves I can handle experiment infrastructure, evaluation, and long-horizon technical work."
- "This AI Hub role is the applied-AI continuation of work I am already doing well."

## Why This Is a PhD Step
This role matters beyond summer funding because it can create all four of the assets a PhD application actually needs:

1. **Faculty confidence**
   A funded AI Hub role increases exposure to how you execute under real deadlines, real grant constraints, and real institutional expectations.

2. **Research continuity**
   It links your current quantum/AI work to broader applied AI and data science problems instead of leaving your profile split across disconnected projects.

3. **Better letters and sponsorship**
   The closer you are to trusted, visible delivery, the stronger future faculty advocacy becomes.

4. **PhD-ready narrative**
   The story becomes coherent:
   quantum/AI research + reproducible applied data science + fairness-aware evaluation + internal RIT research trust.

This is the right bridge if the long-term target is not just employment, but funded doctoral research in AI, data science, or adjacent quantum/ML systems.

## Application Strategy
### Core rule
Lead with applied execution. Keep the PhD angle present, but secondary.

### What the first contact must communicate
- You already work effectively inside RIT research workflows.
- You are technically aligned with Python, data science, evaluation, and reproducibility.
- You can continue through summer and fall.
- You care about applied AI work that produces real, usable outputs.

### What the first contact should not do
- Do not lead with fairness, trauma-informed framing, or long theoretical vision.
- Do not sound like you are asking for a favor because Travis already knows you.
- Do not overload the email with too many attachments or too much story.
- Do not bury the most relevant evidence under broad scholarship language.

### Recommended sequence
1. Send the AI Hub email the same day.
2. Attach the current CV.
3. Reference two high-signal projects and note that the codebase and rerunnable research hub are private but available for walkthrough or access if useful.
4. State summer and fall availability clearly.
5. After sending the application, send a short note to Travis saying you applied and that the fit is strong because it extends the same research discipline you are already building in the GA work.
6. Keep executing strongly in the current GA role. The best follow-up signal is visible continued delivery.

## 30-60-90 Day Conversion Plan
### First 30 days
- Learn the project data sources, evaluation criteria, and reproducibility expectations.
- Become the person who makes messy project state legible.
- Deliver one concrete cleanup or analysis artifact that other people can immediately reuse.

### By 60 days
- Own a repeatable workflow for at least one part of the project: cleaning, EDA, benchmarking, or evaluation.
- Produce documentation that lowers the cost for others to use or trust the work.
- Become associated with reliability, not just ideas.

### By 90 days
- Have at least one visible result that faculty can point to as evidence of strong contribution.
- Identify one research question or tooling direction that naturally extends toward PhD-level work.
- Earn the right to discuss longer-horizon continuation from a position of proven delivery.

## Risks to Avoid
- Do not make the first email too long.
- Do not lead with the advisor relationship.
- Do not present yourself as a fairness specialist first and an applied data scientist second.
- Do not sound entitled because you are already a GA.
- Do not send a scattered portfolio.
- Do not make the role sound like it only matters because you need funding.
