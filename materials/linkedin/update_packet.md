### Diabetic Readmission Prediction Using Machine Learning
Foundational healthcare analytics project using the UCI diabetic readmission dataset from 130 U.S. hospitals to predict early readmission.

The project includes preprocessing, feature engineering, and predictive modeling across logistic regression, decision trees, and random forests to improve early-readmission prediction performance. It is the earliest machine-learning piece of the Puzzle Plan's diagnostic decision-support arc and establishes the healthcare analytics foundation for later equity-aware clinical AI work.

### RNA Secondary Structure Prediction and Visualization: Implementing the Nussinov Algorithm with MFE Enhancements
Computational biology project implementing an enhanced Nussinov pipeline with thermodynamic optimization, interactive visualizations, and benchmark-based validation.

The work integrates ViennaRNA-style MFE scoring, Turner energy parameters, and environmental corrections to improve RNA structure-prediction performance while preserving rigorous validation against Rfam sequences, PDB structures, and RNAfold benchmarks. It provides the computational RNA foundation later expanded into fairness-aware bioinformatics.

### Equitable Bioinformatics: Enhancing Diagnostic Decision-Making through RNA and Biomarker Data
Fairness-aware bioinformatics project connecting RNA structure prediction, biomarker-informed diagnostics, and algorithmic accountability.

The project introduces fairness assessment, constrained optimization, and equity-aware evaluation into RNA-centered diagnostic workflows, treating fairness, equity, and performance as joint design goals rather than separate concerns. It establishes the bridge between computational biology and diagnostic equity that later expands into EQUITAS and clinical decision-making research.

### EQUITAS Framework
Cross-institutional framework for equitable diagnostic AI developed through RIT and University of Rochester research on healthcare analytics, fairness-aware bioinformatics, disability-informed design, and educational equity.

Grounded in ethics research on diagnostic AI, fairness-aware bioinformatics work, UofR inclusion and disability scholarship, and public-facing translation materials, EQUITAS treats equity as enforceable infrastructure: community authority, binding governance, fairness validation, and civic literacy for high-stakes clinical and educational systems.

### Fairness-Aware Decision-Making in Quantum Routing and Clinical Settings via Multi-Armed, Contextual, and Informative Bandit Methods
Research project developing a practical, reproducible framework for fairness-aware sequential decision-making in quantum routing and clinical settings using multi-armed, contextual, and informative bandit methods.

The work implements and evaluates bandit policies together with fairness-aware strategies such as disparity reporting over time, fairness-regularized updates, group-aware calibration, and missingness-aware context augmentation. It studies how these interventions affect utility, regret, and group disparities in both clinical simulations and quantum-network routing under uncertainty, limited context, and non-stationarity.

### Threat-Aware Evaluation of Quantum Entanglement Routing and Qubit Allocation via Hybrid Contextual Bandits
Research framework for adversarial quantum entanglement routing, online path selection, and qubit allocation using hybrid contextual bandits and neural decision methods.

The project integrates benchmark quantum-network testbeds into a shared evaluation pipeline for reproducible experiments under stochastic and adversarial conditions, supporting fairness-aware routing decisions that aim to improve efficiency, robustness, validation quality, and service equity together.
